📊 Analysis

The recursive Fibonacci has O(2^n) time complexity. Each call spawns two sub-calls (fibr(n-1) + fibr(n-2)), leading to exponential growth. At n=42, a single call takes ~379 milliseconds (~16.3 million times slower than iterative).

The iterative Fibonacci has O(n) time complexity. It uses a simple loop with three variables, completing in nanoseconds even for n=46. The time stays nearly flat because the loop body is trivial and fits entirely in CPU registers.

The ratio (recursive ÷ iterative) grows exponentially, driven by the ~1.618× branching factor (the golden ratio φ). This dramatically illustrates the cost of redundant recomputation in the naive recursive approach — memoization or iteration eliminates it entirely.
